The 2026 Landscape

CRE Is Drowning in Unstructured Data

Commercial Real Estate generates enormous volumes of unstructured data—lease documents, offering memorandums, rent rolls, zoning filings, environmental reports, and property condition assessments. A single acquisition target might require reviewing 500+ pages of documentation. Yet the industry's analytical workflows still revolve around Excel models populated by analysts manually reading PDFs.

The speed gap is widening. Institutional buyers and PE-backed platforms are deploying AI to screen 100 deals in the time it takes a traditional brokerage to underwrite one. A 2025 CBRE Technology Survey found that firms using AI-assisted underwriting close deals 40% faster than those relying on manual processes. In a market where off-market deal flow determines success, speed is not a luxury—it's survival.

Lease complexity is increasing. Modern commercial leases contain hundreds of clauses covering escalation schedules, co-tenancy triggers, SNDA agreements, CAM reconciliation methodologies, and force majeure provisions. Missing a single clause during due diligence can cost millions. Yet lease abstraction is still performed manually by paralegals, taking 2-4 hours per lease.

Diagnosis

Four Bottlenecks Costing CRE Firms Millions

description

Manual Lease Abstraction

Each commercial lease requires extraction of 40-60 key data points: base rent, escalation schedules, option dates, CAM provisions, co-tenancy clauses, and termination rights. At 2-4 hours per lease, a 200-unit acquisition requires 400-800 analyst hours just for abstraction. Errors in this process—a missed escalation date or misread co-tenancy trigger—can result in six-figure valuation mistakes that aren't discovered until post-acquisition, when they directly impact returns.

table_chart

Slow Underwriting Cycles

The typical CRE underwriting process takes 3-5 days per deal: 2 days gathering data from the OM and rent roll, half a day entering it into the Excel model, and another day running scenarios and sensitivity analyses. This pace means your acquisitions team can evaluate perhaps 8-10 deals per month, while AI-equipped competitors are screening 50+. In competitive bid situations, the firm that can submit a credible LOI within 48 hours of receiving the OM has a decisive advantage.

search_off

Missed Off-Market Signals

The best CRE deals never hit the open market. They're signaled by permit filings for major renovations, corporate layoff announcements that suggest office space dispositions, tax delinquency notices, and ownership entity changes in county records. These signals are publicly available but scattered across dozens of municipal databases and filing systems. Without automated surveillance, brokers rely on personal networks and word-of-mouth—a fundamentally unscalable approach to deal origination.

trending_down

Asset Management Blind Spots

Once acquired, asset managers must track lease expirations, tenant credit quality, market comp movements, and capital expenditure budgets across their entire portfolio. This data lives in disconnected property management software, accounting systems, and personal spreadsheets. Without a unified view, asset managers miss early warning signs of tenant distress, fail to initiate lease renewal negotiations at optimal timing, and cannot quickly model the portfolio-level impact of market shifts like rising cap rates or tenant defaults.

Agentic Architecture

Systems We Deploy for CRE Firms

Agent 1

Automated Lease Abstraction Engine

Our LLM, fine-tuned on CRE legal language, reads entire lease documents and extracts all critical data points: base rent, percentage rent triggers, escalation schedules, option exercise dates, co-tenancy provisions, SNDA clauses, CAM reconciliation methods, and termination rights. The system handles amendments and addendums, linking modifications to the original lease terms. It outputs structured data directly into your underwriting model or asset management database. What took 2-4 hours per lease now takes under 3 minutes, with 97% accuracy on key financial terms.

Agent 2

Algorithmic Underwriting Assistant

Feed our system an Offering Memorandum PDF and a rent roll, and within minutes it extracts all financial data, normalizes it to your firm's format, populates your proprietary Excel underwriting model, and generates a preliminary investment memo with key assumptions, risk flags, and comparable transaction analysis. The analyst then reviews and adjusts assumptions rather than spending 2 days on data entry. This increases deal evaluation capacity by 5-8x, allowing your team to pursue opportunities they would have previously passed on due to bandwidth constraints.

Agent 3

Off-Market Deal Signal Engine

Autonomous agents continuously monitor zoning board minutes, building permit applications, tax assessment changes, corporate SEC filings, bankruptcy court records, and commercial listing databases across your target markets. When signals converge—a major tenant announces layoffs AND the building owner files for a tax reassessment AND a renovation permit was recently pulled—the system identifies high-probability disposition opportunities and alerts your acquisitions team with a pre-built property profile including ownership history, debt maturity estimates, and comparable sales data.

Agent 4

Portfolio Intelligence Dashboard

A unified control center that aggregates data from Yardi, MRI, or AppFolio with market data feeds to provide real-time portfolio analytics. Track weighted average lease term (WALT), tenant credit risk scores, occupancy trends, and NOI performance across your entire portfolio on a single screen. The system automatically flags upcoming lease expirations 12 months in advance, alerts to market rent movements that create above-market or below-market exposure, and models the impact of rising cap rates or tenant defaults on portfolio valuation.
