The 2026 Landscape

Healthcare's Administrative Crisis Has Reached a Breaking Point

The U.S. spends an estimated $812 billion annually on healthcare administration—roughly 30% of total healthcare expenditure, according to a 2025 JAMA study. For individual practices, this translates to front-desk staff drowning in insurance verification calls, providers spending "pajama time" completing notes after hours, and billing teams fighting payer denials that could have been prevented with proper documentation upfront.

Provider burnout is a clinical crisis. A 2025 Medscape report found that 53% of physicians report burnout, with EHR documentation cited as the number one contributor. Physicians spend an average of 15.6 hours per week on documentation outside of patient encounters. This "pajama time" directly correlates with reduced patient panel sizes, higher turnover, and lower quality of care.

Revenue cycle leakage is accelerating. The average practice loses 5-7% of potential revenue to preventable claim denials, slow pre-authorization processing, and under-coding. With payer rules becoming more complex (CMS updated 394 billing policies in 2025 alone), manual coding teams cannot keep pace.

Patient expectations have shifted. Patients now expect digital-first experiences—online scheduling, pre-visit intake via mobile, real-time insurance verification, and post-visit summaries delivered to their portal. Practices that cannot deliver this lose patients to competitors who can.

Diagnosis

The Four Systems Failing Your Practice

edit_note

Documentation Overload

Physicians spend an average of 16 minutes per patient encounter on EHR documentation. For a provider seeing 25 patients per day, that's 6.7 hours of typing—more time than actual patient interaction. The documentation burden is the primary driver of physician burnout, directly reducing the number of patients a practice can see and the quality of care delivered. Current EHR templates are rigid, requiring physicians to navigate through dozens of clicks and dropdowns to document a straightforward visit.

money_off

Revenue Cycle Leakage

The average practice experiences a 12-15% initial claim denial rate. Each denied claim costs $25-$118 to rework, and 60% of denied claims are never resubmitted—representing pure revenue loss. The root causes are predictable and preventable: missing prior authorizations, insufficient documentation of medical necessity, incorrect modifier usage, and outdated payer-specific coding rules. Yet billing teams discover these issues only after the claim has been rejected, 30-60 days post-service.

call

Pre-Authorization Bottleneck

A single prior authorization request requires an average of 45 minutes of staff time: gathering clinical documentation, navigating payer portals, completing forms, and following up on pending requests. For practices submitting 20+ pre-auth requests per day, this consumes 1-2 full-time staff members. Meanwhile, patients wait days or weeks for approval, delaying treatment and damaging satisfaction. In 2025, the AMA reported that 94% of physicians said prior authorization delays lead to negative patient outcomes.

person_add

Patient Intake Friction

New patients still arrive to clipboards and paper forms. Staff then manually key this information into the EHR—a process that takes 12-20 minutes per patient, introduces transcription errors, and creates a bottleneck that backs up the entire appointment schedule. Insurance verification is performed via phone call to the payer, taking 8-15 minutes per patient. These manual intake processes mean your waiting room is always behind schedule and your front desk can never keep up.

Use Cases

Systems We Deploy for Medical Practices

Agent 1

Ambient Clinical Documentation

Our ambient AI agent listens to patient-provider conversations through a secure, edge-processed audio pipeline. It captures the clinical narrative in real-time and automatically generates structured SOAP notes (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) formatted for your specific EHR template. The provider reviews and approves the note with a single click, rather than spending 16 minutes typing. All audio processing occurs on-premise or in your private cloud—no PHI ever touches a public API. Providers using this system reclaim an average of 2-3 hours per day, translating to 4-6 additional patient slots per week.

Agent 2

Predictive Claim Scrubbing Engine

Before a claim leaves your billing system, our LLM-powered scrubbing agent analyzes the clinical documentation against payer-specific guidelines, LCD/NCD policies, and historical denial patterns. It checks that medical necessity is sufficiently documented, modifiers are correctly applied, diagnosis codes support the procedures billed, and all required authorizations are on file. Claims that would be denied are flagged with specific remediation instructions before submission. This shifts denial management from reactive rework (costing $25-$118 per claim) to proactive prevention, reducing denial rates by 60-80%.

Agent 3

Autonomous Pre-Authorization Workflow

When a provider orders a procedure requiring pre-auth, our agent automatically pulls the required clinical evidence from the patient's chart (lab results, imaging reports, prior visit notes), determines the correct payer-specific form and submission channel, completes the authorization request, and submits it electronically. For payers that don't support electronic submission, the agent generates fax-ready documentation. It then monitors the request status and escalates stalled approvals. This reduces pre-auth turnaround from 3-5 days to same-day for 70%+ of requests, and eliminates 45 minutes of staff time per request.

Agent 4

Intelligent Patient Intake & Engagement

Patients complete intake forms on their mobile device before arriving. Our system automatically verifies insurance eligibility in real-time via EDI 270/271 transactions, calculates estimated patient responsibility, collects co-pays digitally, and pre-populates the EHR record—all before the patient walks through the door. Post-visit, the agent sends personalized care instructions, medication reminders, and follow-up scheduling prompts. This eliminates clipboard intake entirely, reduces check-in time to under 2 minutes, and increases patient satisfaction scores by 25-40%.
