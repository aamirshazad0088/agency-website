Background

About EchoCommerce

EchoCommerce is a direct-to-consumer lifestyle brand selling premium home goods across the US and Canada. Founded in 2019 by David Lin, they grew from a Shopify storefront to a $12M ARR operation by 2023, primarily through Instagram and TikTok-driven acquisition.

Their rapid growth was a double-edged sword. Monthly order volume surged from 800 to 8,000+ in 18 months. Customer support tickets scaled proportionally—but their 4-person support team couldn't. By Q3 2023, average first-response time had ballooned to 24 hours, and their Trustpilot rating dropped from 4.7 to 3.9 stars.

"We were hemorrhaging customers because of support wait times. Every bad review mentioned the same thing: 'Great products, terrible support.' We either needed to hire 8 more agents at $320K/year—or find a completely different approach."

— David L., Founder & CEO

Diagnosis

The Problem: Linear Scaling Trap

Our ticket audit revealed that the majority of support volume was predictable, repetitive, and solvable—if the right systems were in place.

schedule

24-Hour Response Time

Customers submitting tickets on Monday evening weren't hearing back until Wednesday morning. For order issues and delivery complaints, this delay was converting one-time buyers into one-star reviewers. The support backlog grew by 35% every month.

repeat

73% Repetitive Queries

Our audit of 12,000 historical tickets found that 73% fell into just 8 categories: order tracking (28%), return/exchange requests (18%), shipping delays (12%), product questions (8%), and four other common types. Human agents were writing the same responses hundreds of times per week.

lan

4 Disconnected Systems

To resolve a single return request, an agent needed to check Shopify (order status), ShipBob (tracking), Loop Returns (RMA policy), and Klaviyo (customer history). Each lookup took 2-4 minutes. A "simple" return took 12-18 minutes of agent time across four browser tabs.

trending_down

3.9-Star Trustpilot Rating

Down from 4.7 stars in 6 months. Negative reviews specifically cited slow support response as the #1 complaint. The declining rating was directly impacting ad conversion rates—their customer acquisition cost rose 22% as social proof weakened.

Execution

8-Week Deployment Timeline

Deployed alongside live support operations with a progressive traffic routing strategy—zero customer impact during transition.

Week 1-2

Ticket Forensics & Model Training

Analyzed 12,000 historical tickets to map intent taxonomy. Identified the 8 primary categories covering 73% of volume. Extracted 2,400 high-quality agent response examples for fine-tuning. Built the training dataset with edge case coverage for returns involving damaged items, international shipping, and subscription orders.

Week 3-4

Integration Engineering

Built API connectors to Shopify (order data), ShipBob (tracking), Loop Returns (RMA logic), and Klaviyo (customer history). The AI agent could now pull a complete customer context—order history, shipping status, return eligibility, and lifetime value—in under 800ms. Previously this took agents 12-18 minutes of manual lookup.

Week 5-6

Shadow Mode & Quality Gates

Ran the AI agent in shadow mode on live tickets: it generated draft responses for human agents to review but didn't send anything directly. This phase produced 1,840 shadow responses. Human agents rated 94.3% as "send-ready," and the remaining 5.7% were used to further fine-tune the model. We established confidence thresholds: only responses scoring above 0.92 confidence would auto-send.

Week 7-8

Progressive Rollout to Full Autonomy

Week 7: Routed 25% of inbound tickets to autonomous resolution. Week 8: Scaled to 60% after confirming CSAT held steady at 95%. Human agents now only handle complex edge cases, VIP customers, and escalations. The AI processes Tier-1 tickets 24/7 including weekends and holidays—times when human coverage was previously nonexistent.
