Background

About Novus Operations

Novus Operations is a mid-market logistics and supply chain management company headquartered in Austin, Texas. Founded in 2016, they coordinate freight forwarding, warehousing, and last-mile delivery for 340+ e-commerce brands across North America.

By 2023, Novus had grown to 120 employees and was managing $47M in annual throughput. But their rapid growth had exposed a critical weakness: the operational data infrastructure that worked at $5M was buckling under $47M. Every department was running on its own spreadsheet ecosystem, and nobody trusted anyone else's numbers.

"We had 14 different spreadsheets tracking the same shipment data. When the CEO asked for our Q3 delivery accuracy rate, three departments gave three different answers. That was the breaking point."

— Aisha M., Operations Director

Diagnosis

The Problem: Death by Spreadsheet

Our discovery audit revealed a systemic data crisis that was costing Novus far more than they realized.

table_chart

14 Redundant Spreadsheets

Warehouse managers, account executives, and finance each maintained separate tracking sheets. A single shipment was entered manually into an average of 3.2 systems. The ops team spent Monday through Wednesday every week just reconciling data before any actual analysis could begin.

error

23% Data Error Rate

Manual data entry across disconnected systems produced a 23% discrepancy rate in shipment records. This meant that nearly 1 in 4 data points in executive reports was inaccurate—making forecasting essentially unreliable and causing $180K in annual billing disputes.

group_off

3 FTEs on Data Entry

Three full-time employees—costing approximately $195K/year in loaded salary—were dedicated almost entirely to copying data between systems, formatting reports, and manually verifying numbers. These were experienced operations staff doing $15/hr work.

hourglass_top

72-Hour Decision Lag

Because weekly reports took until Wednesday to compile and verify, leadership was making Thursday decisions based on data that was already 3-5 days old. In logistics, where margins depend on real-time routing, this delay was directly eroding profitability.

Execution

6-Week Implementation Timeline

We deployed alongside live operations with zero downtime. Each phase was validated with the Novus team before advancing.

Week 1-2

Discovery & Systems Audit

Mapped all 14 spreadsheet workflows. Interviewed 8 stakeholders across ops, finance, and warehouse teams. Identified 47 unique data fields being tracked (with 19 redundancies). Documented the full data lifecycle from shipment creation to invoice reconciliation.

Week 3

API Integration & Pipeline Build

Built custom REST API connectors for ShipStation, NetSuite, Salesforce, and their proprietary WMS. Designed the ETL pipeline with built-in deduplication logic that resolved the 23% error rate. Implemented automated validation rules that flag anomalies in real-time rather than after the fact.

Week 4

Dashboard Development

Designed 4 role-specific dashboards: Executive Overview, Warehouse Operations, Account Management, and Financial Reconciliation. Each dashboard pulls from the unified data lake with sub-second query response times. Built automated morning digest emails and Slack alerts for threshold breaches.

Week 5-6

Parallel Testing & Handover

Ran the new system in parallel with existing spreadsheets for 2 weeks to validate accuracy. The automated system matched or exceeded manual accuracy in 99.2% of test cases. Conducted hands-on training with 12 team members. Full cutover completed on Day 42 with zero incidents.
